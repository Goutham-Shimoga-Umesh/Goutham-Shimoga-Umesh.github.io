Flying Saucer — Angle Sweep (3D External Aero)

Effect of tilt angle (θ = 0° / 25° / 50°) on flow features & forces
3DExternal Aero
ANSYS Fluent · k–ω SST · symmetry plane

Objective. Quantify how tilting a saucer-like body changes separation pattern and the relative lift/drag behaviour.

Setup. Uniform inlet; k–ω SST; symmetry plane through the body. Three attitudes were tested: θ = 0°, 25°, 50°.

Angle sweep — qualitative trends

Angle Jet/recirculation Wake width Separation extent Relative lift trend* Relative drag trend*
θ = 0° Jet symmetric, attached fore-body Narrow Mild Low Low
θ = 25° Jet deflects downward; larger recirculation bubble Moderate Moderate Medium Medium
θ = 50° Strong downward jet; separated shear layer grows Wide Large Higher Higher

*Relative trends inferred visually from the field plots and separation growth (no speculative numbers).

Notes & interpretation

  • As θ increases, the separated shear layer thickens and the recirculation region elongates, consistent with rising drag.
  • Downward jet deflection with angle suggests an increasing normal force (qualitative lift increase).
  • k–ω SST is appropriate here to capture adverse-pressure-gradient separation with reasonable robustness.
