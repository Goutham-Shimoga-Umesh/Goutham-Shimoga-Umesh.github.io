Flow Over Cylinder — Vortex Shedding (2D)

Unsteady bluff-body wake simulation to quantify shedding characteristics (lift waveform, period, Strouhal) and compare against two elliptical variants.

Objective

Resolve the von Kármán vortex street behind a 2-D cylinder and extract oscillation metrics (period, frequency, Strouhal). Assess how cross-sectional shape (ellipses) alters lift amplitude and period.

Setup

Software / VersionANSYS Fluent 2023 R1 (Teaching)
PhysicsLaminar, transient; incompressible diesel (ρ = 730 kg·m⁻³, μ = 0.0024 kg·m⁻¹·s⁻¹)
Geometry2-D channel with obstacle; circular cylinder D = 0.04 m; ellipse variants 5×3 cm
BCsVelocity inlet U = 0.05 m·s⁻¹, pressure outlet p = 0, top/bottom zero-shear, cylinder no-slip
Mesh / Time≈56k cells; Δt = 0.05 s; max 60 iters/step; total time 60 s (~1200 steps)
Reynolds numberRe ≈ 730·0.05·0.04 / 0.0024 ≈ 608

Validation & Checks

  • Post-spin-up lift signal reaches a stable limit cycle.
  • Computed Strouhal St ≈ 0.219 (within typical 0.18–0.22 range for Re ≈ 600).

Insight

The mesh/time resolution captures a stable Kármán street at Re≈608 with St≈0.22. Geometry strongly modulates lift: a y-elongated ellipse amplifies unsteady loading (~1.6× circular), while x-elongation damps it (~0.3×).
