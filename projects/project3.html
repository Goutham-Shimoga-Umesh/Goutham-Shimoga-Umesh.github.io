Pentagon Building — Crosswind Loading (3D)
LinkedIn Email Resume (PDF) GitHub
3D Bluff Body ANSYS Fluent
Tools: SpaceClaim/CATIA • Meshing • k–ω SST • Post-processing

Overview

Objective: quantify crosswind loading on a pentagon-shaped building by decomposing drag into pressure and viscous components. Two runs were evaluated to compare the wake structure and near-wall loading; results are reported on the symmetry plane.

Setup (concise)

  • Geometry: pentagon prism, free-stream domain sized to minimize blockage.
  • Inlet: uniform inflow (V ≈ 0.8 z tunnel scale); Outlet: pressure outlet.
  • Walls: no-slip; building faces capture pressure drag, viscous skin friction captured on all walls.
  • Solver: steady RANS, k–ω SST; second-order spatial discretization.
  • Monitors: force reports (total/pressure/viscous drag) + residuals to ensure convergence.

Key outputs

Takeaways

  • Pressure drag dominates the total drag; viscous contribution is comparatively small (skin friction).
  • Run-to-run differences appear as wake length and core strength changes in y-velocity, consistent with the observed pressure footprint behind the building.
  • Flow features (stagnation at windward face, separation at edges, and bluff-body wake) match expectations for a polygonal cross-section at moderate Reynolds number.
