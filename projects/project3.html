Pentagon Building — Crosswind Loading (3D)

3D Bluff Body ANSYS Fluent

External flow around a pentagon-planform building placed in a wind tunnel. Objective: visualize wake structure and compare pressure vs viscous drag contributions for two inlet profiles.

Setup (brief)

  • Solver: ANSYS Fluent, pressure-based, steady RANS.
  • Turbulence: k–ω SST; standard wall treatment.
  • Geometry: pentagon prism; tunnel H×W with adequate blockage margin; symmetry plane used for plots.
  • Two runs: Run 1 and Run 2 differ by inlet profile / effective shear (per assignment spec).
  • Outputs: y-velocity and static pressure on the symmetry plane + integrated drag breakdown.

Key outputs

Drag breakdown (text summary)

Integrated forces over the building show that pressure drag dominates the total drag for both runs; the viscous (skin-friction) contribution is negligible relative to the pressure term. This is consistent with bluff-body behavior at the tested Reynolds number.

  • Run 1: total drag ≈ pressure drag (viscous term « small »).
  • Run 2: total drag increases slightly vs Run 1, driven primarily by higher pressure drag.

Exact numeric values are available in the Fluent force reports and can be added on request.
