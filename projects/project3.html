Pentagon Building — Crosswind Loading (3D)

ANSYS Fluent · External Aero · k–ε (steady)

Problem

3-D virtual wind-tunnel study of a pentagon-planform building to compare aerodynamic loading for two orientations: Run 1 — flat face into the wind, and Run 2 — vertex into the wind.

Setup

  • Fluid & model: Air (constant ρ, μ), steady RANS, k–ε (default).
  • Inlet profile: \( V_y = 0.8\,z \) (m/s), giving 0 m/s at ground and 12 m/s at tunnel top.
  • BCs: Velocity inlet (upstream), pressure outlet (downstream), no-slip on ground & building, zero-shear on side & top walls.
  • Mesh: Unstructured volume with local refinement around edges and in the wake.
  • Convergence: Iterated to stabilize forces; reported total drag and pressure/viscous split.

Results — Flow fields

Forces — What matters

  • Pressure drag dominates the total drag in both orientations; viscous (skin-friction) is a small fraction.
  • Vertex-first (Run 2) yields a higher total drag because separation starts earlier and the wake is wider.
  • Flat-face (Run 1) shows a more compact recirculation zone and lower pressure deficit downstream.

Note: I replaced the raw report screenshots with this concise interpretation to keep the page clean. If you’d like, I can add a tidy numeric table later.

Takeaways

  • Planform orientation significantly affects crosswind drag via wake size and pressure deficit.
  • For bluff urban forms, pressure drag reduction hinges on delaying separation (edge rounding, corner treatments).
  • The simple inlet shear profile makes the case reproducible for validation or mesh-sensitivity follow-ups.
