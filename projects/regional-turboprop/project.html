Regional Turboprop — Design Point & Wing FEA

Conceptual sizing → wing structural analysis (ANSYS Workbench)
Conceptual SizingStructural FEA
MTOW 38.5 t · Wing area ≈ 66 m² · Installed power ≈ 9.05 MW

Goal. Pick a feasible design point for a regional turboprop using matching-plot methodology (W/S vs W/P), then build a 3D half-wing and evaluate static strength and stiffness.

Sizing highlights. From mission/performance constraints (takeoff, climb, ceiling, cruise), the trade space pointed to W/S ≈ 585 N/m². For an MTOW ≈ 38.5 t, this yields S ≈ 66 m² and installed power ≈ 9.05 MW (two engines).

FEA setup. Wing skins (sheet alloy), ribs and spars modeled as solid parts, root fixed; load cases derived from lift distribution matching the design point. Post-processing shows deformation and von‑Mises stress envelopes.

Takeaway. Peak equivalent stress localizes near the root; global tip deflection pattern consistent with tapered planform. The figure stack below shows the key frames used on the poster.
